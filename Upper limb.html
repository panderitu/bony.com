Upper Limb

Humerus

The humerus bone is located in the upper arm, between the shoulder joint and the elbow joint.

Radius and Ulna

The forearm consists of two bones, the radius and the ulna, with the ulna is located on the pinky side and the radius on your thumb side.

Carpal

The carpal bones are bones of the wrist that connect the distal aspects of the radial and ulnar bones of the forearm to the bases of the five metacarpal bones of the hand.

Metacarpal

The metacarpals are long, thin bones that are located between the carpal bones in the wrist and the phalanges in the digits. Each is comprised of a base, shaft, and head.

Phalanges

The phalanges are the bones that make up the fingers of the hand and the toes of the foot. There are 56 phalanges in the human body, with fourteen on each hand and foot. Three phalanges are present on each finger and toe, with the exception of the thumb and large toe, which possess only two.

Lower Limb

Femur (thigh bone - the longest bone). tibia and fibula, tarsals (ankle bones - 7 in number), metatarsals (5 in number) and phalanges(digits - 14 number) are the bones of the legs (hind limb). A cup shaped bone called patella cover the knee ventrally (knee cap).

Femur

The femur is the only bone in your thigh. It runs from your hip to your knee.

Tibia and Febula

Tibia and fibula are the two long bones located in the lower leg. The tibia is a larger bone on the inside, and the fibula is a smaller bone on the outside.

Tarsal

The bones of the foot found at the top of the arch, the heel, and the ankle are called the tarsal bones.

Metatarsal

The metatarsals are located in the forefoot, between the tarsals and phalanges. They are numbered I-V (medial to lateral).

Phalanges

The phalanges are the bones that make up the fingers of the hand and the toes of the foot. There are 56 phalanges in the human body, with fourteen on each hand and foot. Three phalanges are present on each finger and toe, with the exception of the thumb and large toe, which possess only two.

Patella

The patella is the largest sesamoid bone in the human body and is located anterior to knee joint within the tendon of the quadriceps femoris muscle.
